Transciptomic Research

Using data publicly available from the Allen Human Brain Atlas and the Julich Cytoarchitectonic Atlas, our lab works to find the connection between gene expression and both structure and function of the human brain. To do so, we manually overlay the two atlases using the common MNI-152 space while employing data-driven methods to uncover transcriptomic correlates of neuroanatomy. Our previous work has revealed genetic gradients that mirror that of the functional hierarchy in the visual system along with changes in the gradient across developmnet. Current research aims to further explore these visual hierarchy genes across the whole cortex along with working towards investigating such interrelatedness across the whole brain and within different functions.


Developmental

In order to understand how the structural organization of the cortex supports cognition, it is important to study how structure-function-behavior relations unfold across development. Using multi-modal neuroimaging techniques, we identify and compare the topography and morphology of anatomical structures such as sulci and gyri from infancy through adolescence. We are particularly interested in (1) the development of sulci and gyri and the positioning of cortical and functional regions, (2) the relationship between sulcal morphology and the development of cognitive skills 3) variability in cortical morphology and its relation to myelination, microstructural properties, and white matter connectivity.

This large-scale structural-functional coupling across age groups may be a foundational building block for high-level visual representations. Image adapted from Weiner et al., 2013.

Our previous work has shown that the topological layout of some large-scale maps is similar in children, adolescents, and adults. We have also found that tertiary sulci such as the mid-fusiform sulcus, are functional landmarks across development. Our current work aims to extend these findings to examine the role of tertiary sulci in other cortical areas.
